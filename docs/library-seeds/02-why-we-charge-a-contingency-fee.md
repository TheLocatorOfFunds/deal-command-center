# Why we charge a contingency fee

Most people who reach out to us have one fair question right up front: why does RefundLocators take a percentage of the recovery? Here's the honest answer.

## The deal in one line

If we don't recover your money, you owe us zero. If we do, we keep a percentage and send you the rest. No bills, no hourly rates, no surprises.

## What you're actually paying for

Recovering surplus funds in Ohio takes:

- **A licensed attorney** who appears in court on your behalf
- **Court filings** (the motion for distribution, claimant affidavits, hearing notices)
- **Probate work**, when the foreclosed homeowner has passed away and heirs need to be sorted out
- **Time** — 90 to 180 days of follow-up with the court, the clerk's office, and any other claimants
- **Notary, copies, certified mail, courthouse fees** — small but real costs that add up
- **The risk** that your case turns out to be more complicated than it looked on paper, and we end up putting in 6 months of work for nothing

When you hire us on contingency, you're paying for a result, not for time. That alignment matters because it means we don't take cases we don't think we can win.

## Why we don't charge hourly

Two reasons.

First, you just lost your house. The last thing you need is another bill arriving every month while a case grinds along for half a year. Hourly billing in this kind of situation is how people end up owing more than they recover.

Second, hourly billing rewards slowness. We'd rather get paid more for doing the work fast than less for stretching it out. Contingency keeps us motivated to close cases.

## Why the percentage isn't smaller

A few of the bigger costs are fixed regardless of recovery size: the attorney's appearance fee, the court filing fee, the notary, the certified mail. On a $20,000 surplus those costs are a meaningful slice. On a $200,000 surplus they're a smaller slice. The percentage we charge averages out to a reasonable margin across the range of cases we take.

We also turn down cases under about $5,000 because the math doesn't work for either of us at that size.

## Q: Can you tell me the exact percentage before I sign?

Yes. It's written into the engagement agreement and we'll quote it before you sign anything. There's no fine print or hidden multiplier.

## Q: Are there any extra fees I'd pay separately?

No. The percentage we quote covers everything: attorney costs, court filings, notary, certified mail, the work itself. If we say 25%, that's what you pay — nothing more.

The one exception is if you specifically ask us to add work outside the surplus claim (for example, helping you find a probate attorney for an unrelated estate matter). We'd quote that separately and you'd decide whether to take it on.

## Q: What if I find the money cheaper elsewhere?

Some companies charge less, some more. Some only do the easy cases and won't take probate-involved files. Some charge hourly, which can run higher than contingency for any case that takes longer than expected.

The honest answer: shop around if you want to. We compete on track record, response speed, and not making your life harder during a tough season. If price is your only criterion, you might find someone cheaper. We're upfront that we're not the cheapest.

## Q: Why not just do it myself?

You can. The process is public and the forms are public. It usually takes 6 to 12 months and a few hundred dollars in court fees, and you'll need to find a probate attorney if there are heirs involved. Some people prefer that route.

We exist for people who'd rather not deal with it. The percentage we charge is what that choice costs.

## Bottom line

We work on contingency because it puts us on the same side of the table as you. We don't get paid until you do. We don't take cases we don't think we can win. We tell you the percentage in writing before you sign. If at any point you want to walk away, you can — there's nothing to pay back.
