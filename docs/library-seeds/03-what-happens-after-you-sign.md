# What happens after you sign the engagement

A clear timeline of what RefundLocators does for you in the 90 to 180 days between when you sign the engagement agreement and when the funds hit your account.

## Day 0 — You sign

You receive the engagement agreement via DocuSign. It's a short document — three or four pages, plain language, with the recovery amount estimate and our fee percentage spelled out clearly. You sign electronically. Takes about 30 seconds.

The same day, we open a personalized portal for you. You'll get a link via email and text. The portal shows your case status in real time and lets you message Nathan directly without having to call.

## Day 1 to 3 — We collect documents

We'll text you a short list of things we need:

- A photo of your driver's license or state ID
- One piece of mail showing your current address (utility bill, lease, or bank statement)
- Any paperwork you received about the foreclosure (sheriff's sale notice, court summons, lender letters)

If the original homeowner has passed away and you're inheriting the right to the funds, we may also need a death certificate and any probate paperwork. We'll walk you through that part.

You can upload the documents through the portal or text them to us. Most clients send everything within 48 hours.

## Day 3 to 14 — We file the claim

Our attorney drafts the formal Motion for Distribution of Surplus Funds and files it with the court that handled your foreclosure. We attach your affidavit confirming identity and right to claim, plus any supporting documentation.

You'll see "Claim filed" in your portal the day this happens. Most cases reach this milestone in the first two weeks.

## Day 14 to 60 — The court reviews

The court schedules a hearing and notifies any other potential claimants (a former spouse, lienholders, heirs in a probate case). This gives anyone else who might have a claim the chance to come forward.

This is the slowest part of the process and the part with the least control on our end. Court calendars vary by county. Hamilton County moves faster than Cuyahoga, for example.

You don't have to do anything during this stretch. We'll send you an update if anything notable happens — a hearing date is set, a competing claim is filed, the judge requests additional information.

## Day 60 to 120 — The hearing

Your attorney appears at the hearing on your behalf. You generally do not need to attend. The hearing usually takes 10 to 30 minutes. The judge reviews the claim, hears from any other parties, and either approves the distribution or sets a follow-up hearing.

Most surplus claims with no competing parties are approved at the first hearing. Cases with multiple heirs or competing claims may need 30 to 60 more days for the court to sort out who gets what percentage.

You'll see "Hearing held" and shortly after either "Distribution ordered" (good news) or "Continued" (just a delay, no problem) in your portal.

## Day 120 to 180 — The clerk releases funds

After the judge signs the order of distribution, the county clerk's office cuts a check. Depending on the county, this takes 2 to 6 weeks. The check is made out to RefundLocators's trust account because we represent you in the court proceedings.

Once we receive the funds, we deposit them, deduct our fee per the engagement agreement, and transfer your share to you within 24 hours. You can choose direct deposit (ACH) or a cashier's check by mail. We'll confirm with you which way you want to receive it before we send.

## After distribution

You'll get a final statement showing the recovery amount, our fee, and your net. You'll also receive a 1099 from us at year-end if your share crosses the IRS reporting threshold (currently $600).

That's it. The relationship doesn't continue beyond the close-out unless you have another property in foreclosure or know someone who does. If you ever do, you have Nathan's direct line.

## Q: What if it takes longer than 180 days?

Most cases close inside the 180-day window. The cases that go longer almost always involve probate (the original homeowner has passed and heirs need to be sorted out by the court). Probate adds 4 to 8 weeks on average. The portal will show you which phase your case is in and how long it's been there.

If a case is sitting somewhere it shouldn't be, we'll tell you exactly why and what we're doing about it. We don't ghost clients.

## Q: What if my case ends up with no funds?

Once or twice a year a case turns out to have less surplus than the public record suggested — usually because there's a junior lien we didn't see at intake. If that happens we'll tell you up front. You don't owe us anything. The engagement agreement is contingency: no recovery, no fee.

## Q: Can I check the status anytime?

Yes. Your portal updates in real time as court events happen. If you want to talk to a human, Nathan answers his phone: (513) 516-2306. Texts go straight to him too. Most clients hear back the same business day.

## Q: I changed my mind. Can I cancel?

Yes, any time, with no penalty. If you cancel before we file the claim, you owe nothing. If you cancel after we've filed, you owe nothing — we'd just withdraw the motion. The engagement agreement explains this in the cancellation section.

## Bottom line

We do the legal work, the paperwork, and the chasing. You sign once, send us a few documents, and wait for your portal to show "funds disbursed." Most clients spend less than an hour total on their case. Then we send you the money.
